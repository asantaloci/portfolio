INTRO A lookbook proposal for Urban Outfitters using the November 2018 fashion collection.

Duration: 3 weeks

Year: 2019

Role: UX Designer


Objective

I was assigned to develop a creative change to Urban Outfitter's current website by either adding a feature, a page item, or user functionality that would benefit the experience of the customer.

Summary

This is a high-fidelity wireframe for a Lookbook proposal for Urban Outfitters. It consists of fashion currently found on the website (November, 2018), but is presented in a more unique way. I redesigned current images found on the website and added illustrations, text, and color to the showcasing of the items present.

Research

I did a lot of research on Urban Outfitters current website, as well as their archived websites to see what I could pull together for a redesigned page on their website. They no longer have the lookbook page on their website, yet I found it to be a very artistic and creative way to display the apparel and other goods that Urban Outfitters has to offer. It was an element on the website that could have more artistic exploration in web design and fashion as well, compared to the rest of the website that is purely used for selling specific products.

Development

I played around with the lettering and the shapes around the photos, but I kept the focus on the outfits and placed the descriptions of the items next to the images. I selected images not only because of their fit to Urban's theme (since it is their own clothing I'm presenting), but also to showcase an example of what a revamped lookbook could potentially look like in the future.




Interface Design

Conclusion and Next Steps

This was a fun project that allowed me to pull current content and put my own unique spin onto its design, specifically the Urban Outfitter website. I got really good feedback from Urban Outfitters when I presented my work, and they liked that I kept the vibe and heart of Urban active throughout the piece.
