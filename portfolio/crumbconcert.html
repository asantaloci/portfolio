INTRO An eccentric website to host Cat's Cradle concert details for the band Crumb.

Duration: 2 weeks

Year: 2019

Role: Designer, Developer


Objective

The prompt of the project created was to develop a website that would host a live Twitter feed, a Google API, and Youtube API. I decided to revolve the website around one of my favorite bands (Crumb)

Solution

Since I was going to the concert, I decided to create a mock up website for the specific Cat's Cradle performance, including information like the location of the concert, the what people had to say about the event, and some of their music videos on Youtube.

In terms of the design of the site, I decided to present the information in the way I thought the band would've liked best - eccentric. From the colorful, loud music video playing when you first open the page to the cool, quieter tones of the music video and map pages, I tried to resemble Crumb's music itself within my design of the site. The people who knew the band in my few circles of friends thought it represented the band well, which is why I decided to keep my design choices the way they were, to cater and communicate to the audience of Crumb listeners.

Some of things I had to learn included:

- how to host a site on Heroku

- how Google's APIs worked (for the Youtube videos and for Google Maps)

- how to pull live data from Twitter using Twitter API and Heroku's live refresh

I was able to easily configure the Google Maps API onto my site by following the Google API instructions they had, and I was able to customize the design to fit the dark-mode, indie mood of the site.

In terms of the Youtube videos being able to be displayed, this required more research into the Youtube API and fiddling with some of the criteria in order to present the music videos that best represented Crumb. For Twitter, this required the assistance of my Interactive media professor to help me with configuring the Twitter API into my site, as well as figuring out how to pinpoint specific tweets I wanted as well as update them in real-time (done through Heroku).

Here's the final site.

Conclusion and Next Steps

Overall, this was a fun project that allowed me to have a lot of creative wiggle room since it was meant for a indie audience, and I got to learn about many APIs and how to integrate them into a site for the first time.
